Hyperion Drone Team — UAS Development

Team Leader & Founding Member • 2023 — Present

Hyperion is a student UAS development team at the University of the Peloponnese in Patras, Greece. The team designs and builds custom multirotor systems from scratch, giving students direct exposure to R&D, integration and structured testing.

Within its first six months, the team completed “AURORA”, a quadcopter capable of around 30 minutes of flight and up to 10 kg payload. Since then, we have iterated the platform, enhanced its stability and broadened its capabilities through competitions and continuous feedback.

  • Defined the initial mission concept focusing on fire‑fighting and emergency support.
  • Contributed to architecture decisions and flight‑controller selection through hands‑on evaluation.
  • Coordinated build and test activities with attention to safety and repeatability.
  • Helped develop a YOLO‑based AI detection system capable of identifying human targets at long range.
  • Participated in IMAV 2023 (Aachen) preparations and in local innovation competitions, earning awards in entrepreneurship and green innovation.
  • Supported improvements in test ergonomics (PID tuning workflow, RC display integration, dedicated PID test rig).
  • Involved in sponsorship outreach, contact with industry experts and participation in defense and tech events.

The current version, “AURORA 3”, is entering a new test campaign focusing on autonomy, energy efficiency and readiness for future competitions such as IMAV 2026 in France.

HDT‑333 — VTOL Cargo UAV

Diploma Thesis • Concept, Design & Prototype

The HDT‑333 is a VTOL cargo UAV designed for parcel delivery and same‑day logistics applications. The work covered system architecture, structural and aerodynamic design, performance estimation and a 1:6‑scale prototype to validate key assumptions.

The concept combines a tandem‑wing configuration, hybrid propulsion and a modular cargo bay, aiming to offer efficient, safe and flexible logistics operations.

Configuration
VTOL with tandem‑wing layout for urban and interurban missions.
Dimensions
≈3 m overall length, 2.3 m span per wing, 1.92 m² total wing area.
Structure
Carbon‑fibre (Twill 3K) primary structure for stiffness and low weight.
Propulsion
Hybrid system: 23 kW gasoline generator + 24S HV Li‑ion packs.
Motor layout
Nine motors: 8 lift motors (octocopter) + 1 rear cruise motor.
Payload
50 kg payload, ≈0.42 m³ internal cargo volume.
Performance
MTOW 240 kg, ≈190 kg empty, ≈100 km/h cruise, ≈67 min endurance.
Safety
High climb rate, dual parachute system, Pixhawk‑6C and autonomous navigation.

Infinity Touch — Touching the Stratosphere

Experimental Capsule • 2021

Infinity Touch was an experimental high‑altitude project aiming to reach the lower stratosphere with a helium weather balloon and a custom capsule. Several capsule iterations refined internal layout, equipment integration and overall reliability.

  • Developed multiple capsule designs to accommodate cameras, power supplies and communication hardware.
  • Integrated cameras to capture live footage and mission data throughout the ascent.
  • Used a dedicated power and communication setup for tracking and data relay.
  • Designed a custom parachute triggered solely by aerodynamic forces during descent.
  • Prepared a flight plan and coordinated flight permissions with the Hellenic Civil Aviation Authority (HCAA).

On 17 October 2021, the capsule reached an altitude of about 30 km over a 2.5‑hour flight. After crossing the Corinthian Gulf, it safely landed in the sea near Antikyra, validating the overall concept and planning.
