Hyperion Drone Team — UAS Development

Team leader & founding member • 2023 — Present

Hyperion is a student UAS development team at the University of the Peloponnese in Patras, Greece. The team designs and builds custom multirotor drones from scratch, giving students hands‑on exposure to R&D, integration and flight testing alongside their studies.

Within the first six months we designed and built our first drone, “AURORA”, without previous experience, and since then we have been iterating both the platform and our processes through competitions and continuous testing.

  • Defined the initial mission concept focusing on fire‑fighting and emergency‑support scenarios.
  • Evaluated different hardware options (microcontrollers and commercial flight controllers) and selected the most suitable architecture.
  • Built “AURORA”, a quadcopter capable of flying about 30 minutes and carrying roughly 10 kg of payload.
  • Developed a YOLO‑based AI detection system, trained on 3,000+ images, able to detect human‑shaped targets at ~40 m during IMAV 2023 preparation.
  • Earned 1st place in the Entrepreneurship and Innovation Competition (ICT category) and 2nd place in the Green Ideathon (2023).
  • Built two new AURORA versions and a trial product concept (“Sonic Boom”) to improve production and system engineering.
  • Improved test ergonomics (PID tuning workflow, use of an RC screen instead of a laptop in the field, dedicated PID test rig).
  • Secured new sponsorships and strategic partners and expanded our network with industry experts and faculty.
  • Prepared “AURORA 3” for a new test campaign on autonomy and energy consumption while exploring participation in IMAV 2026 in France.

HDT‑333 — VTOL Cargo UAV

Diploma thesis • Concept, design & prototype

The HDT‑333 is a vertical take‑off and landing (VTOL) cargo UAV designed for high‑efficiency parcel delivery and same‑day logistics. The project covered system architecture, hybrid propulsion design, full‑scale performance estimation and the development of a 1:6‑scale prototype to validate flight behaviour and subsystem integration.

The work combined aerodynamic analysis, carbon‑fibre structural design and techno‑economic evaluation to assess the operational feasibility of the concept.

  • VTOL configuration with tandem‑wing layout for flexible urban and interurban cargo missions.
  • Overall length ~3 m, carbon‑fibre (Twill 3K) primary structure and 1.92 m² total wing area (2.3 m span per wing).
  • Hybrid propulsion: 23 kW gasoline generator plus 24S HV Li‑ion battery packs (≈14.3 kWh total).
  • Nine‑motor layout with eight lift motors (octocopter) and one rear cruise motor, supporting up to 50 kg payload and ~0.42 m³ cargo volume.
  • Estimated MTOW 240 kg, ~190 kg empty weight, cruise speed ~100 km/h and ~67 minutes endurance under hybrid operation.
  • High climb rate and dual parachute system for safety and emergency recovery.
  • Pixhawk‑6C flight controller with autonomous navigation and safety‑oriented flight modes.

Infinity Touch — Touching the Stratosphere

Experimental capsule • 2021

Infinity Touch was an experimental high‑altitude balloon project aimed at reaching the lower stratosphere with a custom capsule. Over several iterations we refined the internal layout, payload integration and overall reliability, gradually increasing the amount of onboard equipment.

The final capsule, “HAEPHAESTUS II”, carried multiple cameras, communication devices and a custom parachute system designed to deploy purely from aerodynamic forces during descent.

  • Designed and built around five capsule versions with improved internal space management and equipment mounting.
  • Integrated two action cameras and one security camera for live footage and mission documentation.
  • Used a power bank and a mobile phone with SIM card to support data transmission and location tracking.
  • Implemented a helium‑filled weather balloon and a custom‑made parachute for recovery.
  • Prepared a flight plan and obtained permission from the Hellenic Civil Aviation Authority (HCAA).
  • On 17/10/2021 completed a ~2.5‑hour flight reaching ~30 km altitude.
  • After crossing the Corinthian Gulf, the capsule landed safely at sea near Antikyra, validating the concept and planning.
